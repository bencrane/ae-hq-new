State of AE Hiring, Q3 2026: Enterprise Comp Breaks Records as AI-Native Vendors Raid the Old Guard

Median Enterprise OTE crosses $287,000 as Series C application-layer companies out-hire public incumbents for the first quarter on record.
By the Compensation Desk · Data: 27,842 listings, 9,410 companies · 7 min read

The market for Enterprise Account Executives has entered its most seller-friendly quarter since 2021. Across 27,842 live listings tracked by this desk, median Enterprise on-target earnings reached $287,000 — up 11.2% year over year — with the top decile of offers at AI-native vendors now clearing $410,000.

The composition of demand has shifted decisively. Series C and D application-layer companies — Cursor, Sierra, Clay and Writer chief among them — posted 38% more AE openings this quarter than the trailing average, while public infrastructure incumbents held flat. Datadog and Gong, historically net importers of enterprise talent, became net exporters for the first time in the alumni graph's history.

Follow the reps and the story sharpens. Of the 1,847 sellers who departed Gong in the trailing twelve months, 22% landed at Clay, 18% at Sierra, and 14% at Cursor — a migration corridor that did not exist eighteen months ago. "Where reps from a company go next" has become the single most predictive signal of that company's next four quarters, outperforming funding announcements by a wide margin in this desk's back-testing.

Geography tells a quieter story. Remote-eligible enterprise roles fell to 41% of listings, down from 55% at peak, as San Francisco reasserted itself: the city now hosts 34% of all AI-native AE openings, with New York at 26% and Austin holding 11%. Base-to-variable splits remain anchored at 50/50, but accelerator structures above 100% of quota have grown notably richer at Series C.

Equity remains the wildcard. AE offers at Anthropic, Sierra and Cursor now routinely include refresh grants formerly reserved for engineering — a structural concession to a labor market in which the best enterprise sellers behave, and are priced, like scarce technical talent.

The desk's guidance is unchanged: rank the company before the offer. Sales-headcount growth, investor quality and alumni destinations predict a rep's four-year earnings better than the OTE printed on the offer letter. The board below is sorted accordingly.
